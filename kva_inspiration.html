Inspiration

  1. Claude Monet

    Love at first sight, I would say. The works of Claude Monet led to my obsession with the light and cool breeze of Impressionism.
    He was the founder himself of this art syle. Although I base my personal artwork on a realistic style, this is a painting style I admire greatly.
    Just like Monet, I find inspiration in nature and beautiful scenery. He had talent like no other, and I aspire to have his deep knowledge of lighting and colors.

  2. Pierre Auguste Renoir

    I feel the happiest when I look at a Renoir. I wholeheartedly agree with his words, "Why shouldn't art be pretty? There are enough
    unpleasent things in the world." As another master of Impressionism, Renoir was able to capture the essence and femininity of a female face like no other.
    His perfectly loose brushstrokes take me to my favorite place in the world, Paris. I dream of living inside his paintings, they seem perfectly peaceful and enjoyable.

  3. Michelangelo

    Not only the greatest of Renaissance times, but perhaps of all art history, is Michelangelo. At a mere 25 years of age he had taken
    on one of his most famous masterpieces, the David. The inexplicable artistic instinct he had to break a human form free from a slab of marble is awe-inspiring.
    I was fortunate enough to see the David in person, and I stood in awe of every curve of his face, the veins in this arms, and sheer size of the sculpture.
    Having such an accurate knowledge of human anatomy and ability to render it in 3D is a skill I dream of having.
